6   /**
7    * A <code>Premise</code> is the same as a
8    * {@link Fn0 Fn0&lt;Boolean&gt;}, e.g. a <code>true</code>/<code>false</code>
9    * value, or fact, that may or may not change over time. It differs
10   * from a {@link Predicate} in that it depends on the environment
11   * instead of calculating its result based on an input argument.
12   * <p>
13   * Since the result of a <code>Premise</code> is a {@link java.lang.Boolean}, it is
14   * possible assign semantics to a returned <code>null</code> value (e.g. "undecided" or similar),
15   * but you <em>must</em> make
16   * sure that code using such <code>Fn0&lt;Boolean&gt;s</code> that may return <code>null</code>
17   * are in fact handling <code>null</code>s appropriately. In general, a <code>Premise</code> or
18   * <code>Fn0&lt;Boolean&gt;</code> should <em>never</em> return <code>null</code>.
19   * </p>
20   * <p>
21   * This type does not provide anything over {@link Fn0 Fn0&lt;Boolean&gt;}
22   * but being a more specific nominative type, where
23   * appropriate, solely for communication purposes.
24   * </p>
25   *
26   * <h3>Good practice</h3>
27   * One should never accept
28   * <code>Premise</code>s as argument types, but always
29   * implement method signatures to accept {@link Fn0 Fn0&lt;Boolean&gt;}.
30   */
31  public interface Premise extends Fn0<Boolean> {}
